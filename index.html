Mobile Banking App Redesign

Duration
6 months
Team
UX Researcher, 2 Designers, PM
Methods
User Interviews, Usability Testing, Accessibility Audit

Challenge

The existing mobile banking app had a 2.3-star rating and 40% of users abandoned tasks mid-flow. Customer support was receiving 500+ calls daily about app navigation issues. The challenge was to redesign the app to improve usability while maintaining security and compliance requirements.

Research Approach

I conducted a comprehensive research study including:

• User Interviews: 24 participants across different age groups and tech comfort levels

• Usability Testing: Task-based testing with 32 users on key flows

• Accessibility Audit: WCAG 2.1 compliance testing with assistive technology users

• Competitive Analysis: Feature comparison with top 5 banking apps

User Interview Setup

Key Findings

Navigation Confusion

78% of users couldn't find basic features like transfer funds within 3 attempts

Security Anxiety

Users wanted clearer security indicators and explanations of safety measures

Accessibility Barriers

Screen reader users faced significant challenges with form inputs and navigation

Impact

The redesigned app launched with significant improvements:

• App store rating increased from 2.3 to 4.1 stars

• Task completion rate improved by 65%

• Customer support calls reduced by 40%

• 95% improvement in accessibility compliance

E-commerce Checkout Flow Optimization

Duration
4 months
Team
UX Researcher, Designer, Developer, Data Analyst
Methods
A/B Testing, Analytics, User Testing

Challenge

Cart abandonment rate was 73%, significantly higher than industry average of 60%. Revenue loss was estimated at $2.4M annually. The goal was to identify friction points in the checkout process and optimize for conversion.

Research Process

I employed a data-driven approach combining quantitative and qualitative methods:

• Analytics Deep Dive: Heat mapping and funnel analysis

• User Testing: Think-aloud sessions during checkout

• A/B Testing: Iterative testing of design variations

• Post-Purchase Surveys: Understanding successful completion factors

Analytics Dashboard

Key Insights

Form Friction

Users abandoned when faced with 12+ form fields, especially on mobile

Trust Signals

Lack of security badges and unclear return policy caused hesitation

Guest Checkout

Forced account creation led to 45% of abandonment instances

Results

The optimized checkout flow delivered measurable improvements:

• Cart abandonment reduced from 73% to 58%

• Conversion rate increased by 23%

• Mobile checkout completion improved by 35%

• Annual revenue increase of $1.8M

Healthcare Patient Portal

Duration
8 months
Team
UX Researcher, Service Designer, 3 Developers
Methods
Ethnographic Research, Journey Mapping, Co-design

Challenge

Patients struggled to manage their healthcare digitally, with only 15% portal adoption rate. The existing system was fragmented across multiple platforms, creating confusion and missed appointments. The goal was to create a unified, intuitive patient experience.

Research Methodology

Given the sensitive nature of healthcare, I used ethnographic approaches:

• Contextual Inquiries: Observing patients in clinical settings

• Journey Mapping: End-to-end patient experience mapping

• Co-design Sessions: Collaborative design with patients and staff

• Accessibility Testing: Focus on elderly and disabled users

Patient Journey Map

Key Discoveries

Information Overload

Patients felt overwhelmed by medical jargon and complex interfaces

Family Involvement

Caregivers needed access to manage appointments and medications

Mobile-First Need

65% of patients primarily used mobile devices for healthcare tasks

Outcomes

The new patient portal transformed the healthcare experience:

• Portal adoption increased to 67%

• Patient satisfaction scores improved by 42%

• Missed appointments reduced by 28%

• Staff workload decreased by 30% due to self-service adoption

EdTech Learning Platform

Duration
5 months
Team
UX Researcher, Learning Designer, 2 Developers
Methods
Personas, Journey Mapping, Learning Analytics

Challenge

The online learning platform had a 35% course completion rate and students reported feeling disconnected from the learning experience. The platform needed to better support diverse learning styles and improve engagement across different age groups and technical skill levels.

Research Strategy

I focused on understanding the complete learning journey:

• Student Personas: Created 6 detailed personas across age groups

• Learning Analytics: Analyzed engagement patterns and drop-off points

• Diary Studies: Week-long studies of learning habits

• Educator Interviews: Understanding instructor perspectives

Student Persona Development

Research Insights

Motivation Decay

Students lost motivation after week 2 without social connection

Progress Clarity

Learners needed clearer indicators of progress and achievement

Flexible Pacing

One-size-fits-all pacing didn't work for diverse learners

Impact

The redesigned platform significantly improved learning outcomes:

• Course completion rate increased to 68%

• Student satisfaction scores improved by 52%

• Average time spent per session increased by 40%

• Instructor satisfaction with platform tools increased by 35%
